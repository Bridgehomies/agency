<h2>Start with business questions, not vanity topics</h2>
<p>Good content operations begin where sales conversations begin. If prospects repeatedly ask the same question, that question should probably become an article, FAQ block, or comparison page.</p>

<h2>Turn every article into a reusable asset</h2>
<p>One article can support multiple channels when the structure is strong enough. Pull answers into FAQs, create short social snippets, and use summary sections in sales material.</p>

<h2>Metadata is part of the content system</h2>
<p>Your title, excerpt, schema type, and internal linking strategy should be decided while the article is written, not after publishing. That keeps the page coherent for crawlers and for readers.</p>

<h2>Make the conversion path obvious</h2>
<p>Strong editorial pages do not need aggressive calls to action, but they do need a clear next step. If a reader has consumed the article, the next action should be easy to understand and easy to take.</p>
